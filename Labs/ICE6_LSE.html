import numpy as np
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

x = np.array([0, 1, 2, 3])
y = np.array([-1, 0.4, 0.7, 2.1])

plt.plot(x, y, 'o', markersize=10)
plt.grid('on')
plt.show()

We can rewrite the line equation as y = Ab, where

\(A = \begin{bmatrix} x & 1\end{bmatrix} \) and \(b = \begin{bmatrix} m \\ c\end{bmatrix} \). That is

\(y = mx + c = \begin{bmatrix} x & 1\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} m \\ c\end{bmatrix} = Ab\)

With the \(x\) and \(y\) values, we can obtain

\(\begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 0.4 \\ 0.7 \\ 2.1\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 \\2 & 1 \\3 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} m \\ c\end{bmatrix}\)

Now use lstsq to solve for b:

A = np.vstack([x, np.ones(len(x))]).T
print(A)

m, c = np.linalg.lstsq(A, y, rcond=None)[0]
print(m, c)
